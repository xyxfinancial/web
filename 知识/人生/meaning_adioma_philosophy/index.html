    记：哲学 - 生命的意义¶

    起因¶

    一直会寻问自己生命的含义，
也喜欢和他人讨论，
常听到的反馈是
是不是物质/时间充分，
才去想这些有的没的。

但是不是艰难的时候，
才更会思考吗？

我更倾向于觉得，
因为幸福是一种感觉，
它本就是精神世界的内容，
所以想要更多的感到幸福，
了解形而上是必然的。

    各哲学¶

    自然泛神论 (Natural Pantheism)¶

    可追溯到史前时代
泛神论是一种古老的思想，在1675年成为独立的哲学思想
荷兰哲学家 巴鲁克·斯宾诺莎（1632-1677）
# 生命的意义在于与一切和谐相处。
泛神论相信上帝存在于一切事物中，所有事物共同构成了一个包罗万象的上帝

    Dates back to prehistoric times
Pantheism is the belief that God is in everything, that all things together comprise an all-encompassing god. 
# The meaning of life is in living in harmony with all that there is.
Pantheism is an ancient idea that was formalized as a separate philosophy in "Ethics by the Dutch philosopher" by Baruch Spinoza (1632-1677) in 1675.

    有神论 (Theism)¶

    可以追溯到史前时代
有神论可以追溯到人类的曙光，在那里它以各种形式实践，这取决于一群人相信他们的上帝是什么样子。
# 生命的意义由一个人所相信的上帝规定。
有神论是上帝或至高存在存在的命题。

    Dates back to prehistoric times
Theism is the proposition that God or a Supreme Being exists. 
# The meaning of life is then prescribed by the God that one believes in.
Theism dates back to the dawn of humanity where it was practiced in its various forms depending on what a group of humans believed their god to be like.

    道教 (Daoism)¶

    大约公元前
《道德经》作者：中国哲学家老子（生活在公元前6世纪至公元前4世纪）是道教的主要来源。
# 无爲（无刻意行动或自然）
5世纪，道教通过"无为"为人们提供了一种无痛的方式来寻找生命的意义。这样的行动导致找到道，这是"道路"。然而，"道路"无法通过任何解释或行动来理解。道只有在一个人是他自己的时候才会显露出来。

    Circa 5th Century BC
Daoism offers people a painless way of finding life’s meaning through Wu Wei (無爲) “action without intention” or “naturalness”. 
# Such action leads to finding the Dao, which is “the way”. And yet, “the way” cannot be understood through any explanation or action. The Dao reveals itself only when a person simply is.
The  Dao De Jing (aka Tao Te Ching) by Chinese philosopher  Lao Zi (lived c. 6th – 4th century BC) is the primary source on Daoism.

    决定论 (Determinism)¶

    大约公元前
6世纪，决定论认为所有事件都是由于先前存在的原因而发生的。因为在一个预先确定的世界里，没有什么是可以改变的，所以一个人就不可能有自由意志。
# 生命的意义，是有一个的，也是预先确定的，我们什么也做不了去理解它。
决定论的思想是古老的，在18世纪的数学中复兴。其著名代表之一是法国哲学家和数学家勒内·笛卡尔(1596-1650)

    Circa 6th Century BC
Determinism is the idea that all events happen as a result of previously existing causes. Since nothing can be changed in a pre-determined world, a person cannot have free will. 
# The meaning of life, is there is one, is also pre-determined, and we cannot do anything to understand it.
The idea of determinism is ancient, reviving in the mathematics of the 18th. One of its well-known representatives is the French philosopher and mathematician  Rene Descartes (1596-1650).

    儒学 (Confucianism)¶

    约公元前
5世纪中国哲学家孔子（公元前551-479年）告诉我们要培养称为仁的美德，这是一种在照顾孩子和父母时所经历的利他主义的感觉。
孔子解释美德的众多方式之一是：
"现在，具有完美美德的人，希望建立自己，也寻求建立他人;他希望自己被放大，他也寻求扩大他人。能够通过我们自己近在咫尺的东西来判断他人——这可以被称为美德的艺术。"
（论语 6：30）
儒家思想的主要来源是孔子论语

    Circa 5th Century BC
Chinese philosopher  Confucius (551-479 BC) tells us to cultivate virtue called Ren (仁) which is an altruistic sort of feeling one experiences when taking care of one’s children and parents. 
One of many ways Confucius explained virtue is this:
"Now the man of perfect virtue, wishing to be established himself, seeks also to establish others; wishing to be enlarged himself, he seeks also to enlarge others. To be able to judge of others by what is nigh in ourselves – this may be called the art of virtue."
(Analects 6:30)
The primary source on Confucianism is  The Analects of Confucius.

    墨家 (Mohism)¶

    出现在公元前
5世纪左右，墨子主义者提出了"包容的爱"的概念，建爱是一种对人类同胞的公正关怀，包括社会中的每个人。
# 生命的意义在于遵循一种叫"法仪"的模型
在这种模型中，一个人的心理状态和由此产生的有益行为是同一枚硬币的两面。
墨家的来源是墨子，中国哲学家撰写的71本书汇编墨子（约公元前470年 – 公元前391年）。

    Appeared around 5th Century BC
The Mohists propose the concept of “inclusive love” jian ai, a kind of impartial care for fellow human beings that includes everyone in society. 
# The meaning of life is in following the model called Fa (法) in which one’s psychological state of care and the beneficial behavior that results from it are two sides of the same coin.
The source of Mohism is  Mozi, a compilation of 71 books written by the Chinese philosopher  Mozi (c. 470 – 391 BC).

    唯我论 (Solipsism)¶

    唯我主义出现在公元前
5世纪左右，是一种理论，即一个人只能确定一件事：一个人的思想存在。
唯我论最早是由希腊高尔吉亚斯（483-375）说：
1. 什么都不存在。
2. 即使存在某些东西，也无法知道任何事情。
3. 即使可以知道一些关于它的知识，关于它的知识也无法传达给其他人。
# 根据唯我论，生命的意义只能由一个人的思想来认识，而不能与其他众生有关。
一些心理学家认为新生儿最初是唯我论的。嬰兒在父母不在身邊時哭泣，因為他們相信當不被看見時，父母就不再存在。最终，孩子们从观察他人中学会拒绝唯我论。

    Appeared around 5th Century BC
Solipsism is a theory that one can only be certain about one thing: one’s mind to exist. Solipsism was first recorded by the Greek sophist,  Gorgias (483-375) who is reported to have said:
Nothing exists.
Even if something exists, nothing can be known about it.
Even if something could be known about it, knowledge about it can’t be communicated to others.
# The meaning of life according to solipsism can only be known by one’s mind and not in relation to other beings.
Some psychologists believe newborns to be initially solipsistic. Infants cry in the absence of parents nearby because they believe that when not visible, the parents stop existing. Eventually, children learn from observing others to reject solipsism.

    玩世不恭 (Cynicism)¶

    出现在公元前
4世纪左右 愤世嫉俗者试图在不确定的时代为人们提供幸福和免于痛苦的可能性。
# 生命的意义是精神上的清醒和自给自足（eudaimonia）
为了实现自给自足，一个人必须摆脱外部影响 - 例如财富，名声和权力。
在犬儒主义或任何官方教义方面没有中央权威。然而，这在古希腊是一个有影响力的传统。

    Appeared around 4th Century BC
The Cynics attempt to offer people the possibility of happiness and freedom from suffering in the age of uncertainty. 
# The meaning of life is mental lucidity and self-sufficiency (eudaimonia). 
To achieve self-sufficiency, a person must become free from external influences – such as wealth, fame, and power.
There is no central authority on Cynicism or any official doctrine. Yet, it was an influential tradition in Ancient Greece.

    享乐主义 (Hedonism)¶

    出现于公元前
4世纪左右，享乐主义为我们提供了一种基于寻求快乐和避免痛苦的生活。
# 简单的快乐，如吃饭、跳舞和播放音乐，本身就很有意义。
享乐主义是一个古老的思想，后来由希腊哲学家德谟克利特提出(约460 – 370 B.C.)

    Appeared around 4th Century BC
Hedonism offers us a life based on seeking pleasure and avoiding suffering. 
# Simple pleasures like eating, dancing, and playing music are meaningful in themselves.
Hedonism is an ancient idea that was later formulated by the Greek philosopher  Democritus (c. 460 – 370 B.C.).

    柏拉图哲学 (Platonism)¶

    出现在公元前
4世纪左右，为希腊哲学家柏拉图（约公元前428年–约公元前347年），
# 生命的意义就是对知识的追求。
柏拉图在他的书《道歉》中引用了他的老师苏格拉底（约公元前470年 – 公元前399年）的话,说"未经检验的生活不值得过"。
简而言之，柏拉图主义认为存在诸如抽象的"纯粹形式"之类的东西。消融是既不存在于空间中，也不存在于时间中的东西。它完全是非物质的，但它是可知的。
# 对"纯形式"的认识是生命的意义。
尽管听起来令人生畏，但有一种捷径。根据柏拉图的说法，我们生来就拥有我们内心的所有知识，但我们必须回忆它或重新发现它，这是一个叫做"轶事"的概念。
柏拉图最有影响力的作品是共和国出版于公元前375年左右。

    Appeared around 4th Century BC
For the Greek philosopher  Plato (c. 428 – c. 347 BC), the meaning of life is the pursuit of knowledge.  In his book Apology, Plato quotes his teacher  Socrates (c. 470 – 399 BC) saying that “the unexamined life is not worth living”. In a nutshell, Platonism is the idea that there exist such things as “pure forms” which are abstractions. An abstration is something that neither exists in space nor time. It is completely non-physical and yet it is knowable. 
# Knowledge of “pure forms” is the meaning of life. 
Daunting as it sounds, there is a shortcut of sorts. According to Plato, we are all born with all knowledge inside us but we have to recall it or rediscover it, which is a concept called anamnesis.
Plato’s most influential work is  The Republic published around 375 BC.

    律法主义（Legalism)¶

    出现在公元前4世纪左右
律法主义者认为人类天生是自私的，不能被信任为道德行为。一个强大的政府系统可以引导人类继续以自私的方式行事，而整个系统则从他们的工作中受益。
# 生命的意义在于获得技能，使一个人的工作对国家有价值，从而有利于社会。
最早的律法主义文本是商君書由秦朝政治家所寫，將秦提升到亞洲的領袖地位。

    Appeared around 4th Century BC
The Legalists believed that humans are inherently selfish and cannot be trusted to behave morally. A strong government system can steer humans to continue behaving in their selfish ways while the system as a whole benefits from their work. 
# The meaning of life is then in the acquisition of skills that make a person’s work valuable to the state which in turn benefits society.
The earliest Legalist text is  The Book of Lord Shang ( 商君書) written by the politician who raised Qin dynasty to its leading position in Asia.

    伊壁鸠鲁主义 (Epicureanism)¶

    出现在公元前4世纪左右 
希腊哲学家伊壁鸠鲁（公元前341-270年）是一位唯物主義的古希臘哲學家，他提出生命的意義是實現可持續的快樂，這導致了寧靜和免受恐懼的自由（ataraxia）。
# 什么样的快乐是有意义的？精神上的，而不是身体上的，因为精神上的快乐存在于过去、现在和未来，而身体上的快乐是转瞬即逝的。
这首诗"论事物的本质"（De rerum nature）汇编了伊壁鸠鲁主义的核心论点和理论。

    Appeared around 4th Century BC
The Greek philosopher  Epicurus (341-270 BC) was a materialist ancient Greek philosopher who offered that the meaning of life was in achieving sustainable pleasure which leads to a state of tranquility and freedom from fear (ataraxia).
# What sort of pleasures are meaningful? Mental, not physical,  because mental pleasures exist in the past, the present, and the future, while physical pleasures are fleeting.
The poem  On the Nature of Things (De rerum nature)  compiles the core arguments and theories of Epicureanism.

    安静主义 (Quietism)¶

    出现在公元前3世纪左右
安静主义者认为哲学本身没有答案。相反，它的作用是指出向哲学家提出的问题中的语言混乱。
# 因此，生命意义的问题假设我们理解"意义"和"生命"这两个词的含义。任何试图确定任何一个词的含义都揭示了问题的无意义性，因此，提出这样一个问题就无法理解生命的意义。
在东方和西方哲学中都可以找到安静主义的元素，道教以其不愿使用语言而闻名，希腊Pyrrhonism拥抱非语言的"暂停判断"（时代）。

    Appeared around 3rd Century BC
Quietists believe that philosophy as such has no answers to offer. Instead, its role is in pointing out linguistic confusions in the questions presented to philosophers. 
# Thus, the question of the meaning of life assumes that we understand the meaning of the words “meaning” and “life”. Any attempt to pin down the meaning of either word reveals the meaninglessness of the question and thus the meaning of life cannot be understood by asking such a question.
Elements of Quietism are found in both Eastern and Western philosophies with Daoism known for its reluctance to use language and Greek Pyrrhonism embracing non-verbal “suspension of judgment” (epoché).

    亚里士多德主义 (Aristotelianism)¶

    约公元前3世纪
希腊哲学家亚里斯多德（公元前385-322年）提醒我们，没有人为了实现其他目标而过上美好的生活。
# 做一个好人本身就足够了。美德是目标。
没有美德的清单，因为我们都知道它们是什么。
例如，拥有朋友是一种美德。我们不需要被教导这一点。
根据亚里士多德的说法，美德和道德规则不是一个理论概念。一个人知道什么是好的。
亚里士多德的《尼各马可伦理学》是他思想最著名的著作。

    Circa 3rd Century BC
The Greek philosopher  Aristotle (385-322 BC) reminds us that no one lives a good life in order to achieve some other goal. 
# Being a good person in itself is sufficient. 
Virtue is the goal. There is no list of virtues because we all know what they are. For example, it is a virtue to have friends. We do not need to be taught that. Virtue and the rules of ethics are not a theoretical concept according to Aristotle. A human being knows what is good.
Aristotle’s  Nicomachean Ethics is the best known work on his thinking.

    淡泊 (Stoicism)¶

    约公元前3世纪
斯多葛学派（约公元前334年 – 约公元前262年）希望你摆脱对快乐的渴望或对痛苦的恐惧，避免情绪化。
# 一个人如何变得冷静？只有通过智慧，一个人才能自由地公正地行事。一个有智慧的人通过不违反自然法则的理性行动成为圣人。
希腊化哲学家西提姆的芝诺（约公元前334年 – 公元前264年）在公元前300年左右在雅典创立了斯多葛派哲学学派。
唯一完整的斯多葛派作品是塞涅卡,伊壁鸠鲁和罗马皇帝马库斯·奥勒留（121-180）的日记《沉思录》，里面记录了他在自我转变成为圣人方面的进展。

    Circa 3rd Century BC
The Stoics (c. 334 – c. 262 BC) want you to be free from desire for pleasure or fear of pain.  Eschew emotion. 
# How does one become dispassionate? Only through wisdom can one be free to act justly. A wise person becomes a sage through rational action that does not violate the laws of nature.
Hellenistic philosopher  Zeno of Citium (c. 334 – 264 BC) founded the Stoic school of philosophy in Athens about 300 BC. The only complete Stoic works we have are by  Seneca,  Epictetus, and the Roman Emperor  Marcus Aurelius (121–180), whose diary Meditations records his progress on self-transformation toward becoming a sage.

    现代人文主义 (Modern Humanism)¶

    大约在1300年代后期
人文主义指出，人类本身要对这个世界上人类的命运负责。
# 促进和帮助他人是生命的意义。

    Circa the late 1300s
Humanism points out that humans themselves are responsible for the fate of humans in this world. 
# Thus, promoting and helping other humans is the meaning of life.

    主观主义 (Subjectivism)¶

    大约在1600年代初
根据主观主义者的说法，生命的意义因人而异，取决于一个人的精神状态。
# 一个人越是实现自己设定的目标，他们的生活就越有意义。
主观主义者不认同，人们应该不管主观目标去追求生活中可能存在客观价值。
主观主义归因于勒内·笛卡尔和他的思想实验"我认为，因此，我存在。"

    Circa the early 1600s
According to Subjectivists, the meaning of life varies by individual, depending on one’s mental state. 
# The more a person achieves their own goals that are set by themselves, the more meaningful their life is. 
Subjectivists reject that there may be objective values in life that one should achieve despite subjective goals.
Subjectivism is attributed to Rene Descartes and his thought experiment “I think, therefore, I exist.”

    自由主义 (Liberalism)¶

    1689年出现
自由主义者相信，一个人自然可以自由选择做什么，而不需要任何其他人的许可。任何试图限制自由的人都必须首先证明这是必要的。
# 生命的意义在于保护个人自由免受可能合理或可能不合理的政治胁迫。
英国哲学家和医生约翰洛克（1632-1704）的"两部政府论著"是自由主义意识形态的基础文本。

    Appeared in 1689
The Liberalists trust that a person is naturally free to choose what to do without permission from any other person. Anyone attempting to limit freedoms must first prove that it is necessary. 
# The meaning of life is then in protecting individual liberties against the political coercion that may or may not be justified.
English philosopher and physician  John Locke’s (1632-1704) work  Two Treatises of Government is the foundational text of liberal ideology.

    康德主义 (Kantianism)¶

    康德主义出现在1785年
提出每一个人类行为都应该根据普遍的格言或原则来判断。如果一个行为违反了一个原则，那么一个人就辜负了对人类的责任。例如，如果人们遵循这句格言，杀死任何你不喜欢的人，当普遍应用时，它将导致人类的终结。
# 生命的意义在于履行你遵循普遍原则的责任。
康德主义的起源是德国哲学家伊曼纽尔·康德（1724-1804）的书《对纯粹理性的批判》

    Appeared in 1785
Kantianism proposes that every human action should be judged according to a universal maxim, or principle. If an action violates a principle then a person failed their duty toward humans. For example, if people followed the maxim kill anyone you dislike, when applied universally, it would lead to the end of humanity. 
# So, the meaning of life is in fulfilling your duty to follow universal principles.
The origin of Kantianism is German philosopher  Emmanuel Kant’s(1724-1804) book  The Critique of Pure Reason.

    虚无主义 (Nihilism)¶

    1862年出现
# 虚无主义，也被称为悲观主义，是相信没有什么能使生活变得有意义。
虚无主义者看到了人类固有的一些东西，这些东西阻止我们找到生命的意义。它可能是人类不满意的倾向，或者总是在寻找某样东西，或者一旦发现它就感到无聊。
虚无主义的起源是古老的，德国哲学家弗里德里希·尼采（1844-1900）概念"意志权力"最常与之相关。

    Appeared in 1862
# Nihilism, also called Pessimism, is the belief that nothing can make life meaningful. 
The Nihilists see something inherent about humans that prevents us from finding meaning in life. It can be the human tendency for being dissatisfied or always seeking something or being bored once it is found.
The origin of Nihilism is ancient but among philosophers, German philosopher  Friedrich Nietzsche (1844-1900) concept “Will to Power” is most often associated with it.

    实用主义 (Pragmatism)¶

    大约在19世纪70年代
与许多其他哲学流派不同，实用主义者没有官方信条。总的来说，他们的观点表明，我们不应该寻求关于生命的真理，而应该寻求对生活的有用理解。
实用主义的主要人物之一美国哲学家和心理学家威廉·詹姆斯（1842-1910）认为，真理可以被制造，但不能被寻求。
生活值得过吗？詹姆斯回答说："也许吧。答案取决于你在生活中做了什么。"
# 生命的意义在于，在最长的过程中，做对最人性化的益处贡献最大的事情 — 为人类带来最大的价值。
实用主义学说几乎没有主要来源，威廉·詹姆斯是其最多产的作家之一。他的书《威廉·詹姆斯谈习惯、意志、真理和生命的意义》涵盖主题。

    Circa the 1870s
Unlike many other schools of philosophy, the Pragmatists have no official creed. In general, their views suggest that rather than truth about life, we should seek a useful understanding of life.
The American philosopher and psychologist  William James (1842-1910), one of pragmatism’s main figures argued that truth could be made but not sought. Is life worth living? James answered, “Maybe.” The answer depends on what you do with your life. 
# The meaning of life is then doing the thing that most contributes to the most human good over the longest course – that is bringing maximum value to humanity.
There is hardly a main source of the Pragmatism doctrine, but William James is one of its most prolific authors. His book  William James on Habit, Will, Truth, and the Meaning of Life covers the subject.

    逻辑经验主义 (Logical Empiricism)¶

    大约在20世纪20年代
逻辑实证主义也被称为逻辑实证主义，其思想是，我们唯一可用的知识类型是事实 - 科学上可验证和可观察的。其他任何事情都是毫无意义的。
# 生命的意义只能从一个人的实际经验中推导出来。我们无法知道生命是否具有超出我们所能看到的意义。
逻辑实证主义者没有领导者，"维也纳圆环"是该运动最有影响力的团体。

    Circa the 1920s
Also called logical positivism, the idea of logical positivism is that anything that the only type of knowledge available to us is facts – scientifically verifiable and observable. Anything else is meaningless. 
# The meaning of life can then only be derived from one’s actual experience. We cannot know if life has a meaning beyond what we can see.
Although the logical positivists did not have a leader,  The Vienna Circle is the movement’s most influential group.

    存在主义 (Existentialism)¶

    大约在20世纪40年代
存在主义者认为我们都以"存在主义的焦虑"开始生活，一种对我们生活中明显无意义的焦虑感。
# 为了找到生命的意义，一个人必须决定自己的价值观，然后采取行动按照这些价值观生活。
第一个存在主义文本是德国哲学家马丁·海德格尔（1889-1976）的《存在与时间》（1927），进行了对"我们自己是存在"的探索。

    Circa the 1940s
The existentialists think that we all begin life with “existential angst”, a feeling of anxiety about the apparent meaningless of our lives. 
# To find meaning in life, a person has to decide on their own values and then take action to live according to them.
The first existentialist text is German philosopher  Martin Heidegger’s(1889-1976) work  Being and Time (1927), which is an exploration of the “being that we ourselves are”.

    荒谬主义 (Absurdism)¶

    出现在1942年
荒谬主义者问："你为什么还要问这样一个无用的问题？"
这个问题注定要失败，因为拼命寻求意义的人类思想与一个万物都无法拥有有限，不可改变意义的世界之间的冲突。
# 寻找人生的意义是一项系统的任务 — 你越是寻找一个意义，你对它的理解就越少。
西西弗斯成为生命无意义的象征，因为在希腊神话中，他因自己的错误行为而受到惩罚，被迫将一块巨大的巨石推上山坡，但每次他接近山顶时，巨石都会翻滚回来。这种情况持续了很久。
荒谬主义是由法国哲学家阿尔伯特·加缪（1913-1960）提出，人们应该接受我们存在的荒谬性，然后继续刻意过自己的生活。
荒诞主义的主要作品是阿尔伯特·加缪的作品《西西弗斯的神话》

    Appeared in 1942
Absurdists ask, “Why would you even ask such a useless question?” The question destined to fail because of the conflict between the human mind that desperately seeks meaning and a world where everything falls short of having a finite, immutable meaning. 
# Looking for meaning in life is a Sysipean task – the more you search for one, the less you understand it. 
Sysiphus became a symbol of life’s meaninglessness because in Greek mythology he was punished for his misdeeds by being forced to roll an immense boulder up a hill only for it to tumble back every time he neared the summit. This went on for eternity.
The absurdist French philosopher  Albert Camus (1913-1960) proposed that people should embrace the absurdity of our existence and then proceed to wilfully live their lives.
The defining work on absurdism is Albert Camus’s work  The Myth of Sisyphus
